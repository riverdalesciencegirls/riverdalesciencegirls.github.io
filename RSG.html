Bridging the STEM Gender Gap Divide
Throughout history, there has been an overwhelmingly large gender gap between males and females in STEM* professions. Why? According to Andrei Cimpian and Sarah-Jane Leslie from the New York Times, "By the age of 6, young girls are less likely than boys to view their own gender as brilliant...American children are picking up on cultural stereotypes about brilliance at an early age." Despite the fact that girls consistently outperform boys in STEM while in elementary school, they do not believe themselves to be smart or good enough to continue with it.
How can we change this?
Well, one thing we can do is counteract our societal conditioning by encouraging young girls to experience science as fun. Anyone, regardless of age, gender, sexual orientation or race can be successful in the field. This is why we are starting a fun, Saturday science program for girls in the 1st through 6th grades.
Let us unite in the universal passion for Science!
*Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics
