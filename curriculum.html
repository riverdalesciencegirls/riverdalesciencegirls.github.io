Curriculum
Week One: Geology At the start of the lesson, everyone will spend some time introducing themselves. We will then introduce the topic of the day: volcanoes. Cookies will be used to demonstrate the underground plate movement (plate tectonics) and how volcanoes are formed. We will then perform a fun experiment to outline the science behind eruptions.
Week Two: Biology Week two, we will start by digging for earthworms.* Using the scientific method, we will perform a variety of experiments to determine their ideal living conditions. Following the experiments, a few words will be said about the importance of earthworms to the ecosystem.
*The worms will be released safe and sound at the end of the lesson.
Week Three: Chemistry During week three, we will make ice cream in a plastic bag to demonstrate the effects of salt on the freezing point of water.
Week Four: Mechanics This week, we will perform the egg-dropping experiment to explore air resistance. Each student will construct a container to protect their eggs from cracking when they hit the ground. We will talk about how all objects fall at the same rate unless a force, such as air resistance, gets in the way.
Week Five: Chemical Physics This week we will be focused on experimenting with the physics of "oobleck." Oobleck is a non-newtonian fluid: it behaves like both a liquid and a solid. This is a substance that is both easy to make and safe for playing and working with.
Week Six: Quantum Mechanics On the last day of the semester, we will introduce the concept of atoms. Our students will construct a "flour cannon" to learn about how empty space is not actually empty.
